❓ frequently asked questions

How often should I feed my starter?

Once daily at room temperature, or weekly if refrigerated. Adjust based on activity.

What flour gives the best oven spring?

Bread flour with 12-13% protein. Whole wheat can be blended for depth.

Why is my crumb gummy?

Underbaking or slicing too early. Let cool completely.

The rhythm of sourdough: hydration & time

by E. Marchand · 12 min read

Hydration is the soul of sourdough. It dictates the openness of the crumb, the chew, and the crust. But hydration without time is chaos. This guide walks through the interplay of water, flour, and fermentation timing.

Understanding hydration percentages

Baker's percentage: water weight relative to flour. 70% is standard; 80%+ gives a more open crumb but requires stronger gluten development.

HydrationCrumb styleTechnique
65%tight, eveneasy shaping
75%open, irregularcoil folds
85%very opengentle handling

Bulk fermentation: the clock

At 24°C, bulk fermentation takes 4-5 hours. Warmer = faster, but flavour develops slower at cooler temps.

✔ checklist for bulk
  • dough increases 30%
  • visible bubbles on top
  • slight doming
  • windowpane test

    • Cold proofing

    Retard fermentation in the fridge for 12-18 hours. This deepens flavour and makes scoring easier.

    Common mistakes

    • Over-hydrating weak flour
    • Under-proofing (dense crumb)
    • Over-proofing (sour, flat)

    FAQ

    Can I use all-purpose flour?

    Yes, but reduce hydration by 5%.

    How to tell if bulk is done?

    Poke test: indentation springs back slowly.

    Mastering hydration and time transforms your bread from good to transcendent.

    Ancient grains, modern loaves

    by L. Fenwick · 8 min read

    Einkorn, spelt, emmer — these grains are not just novelties. They offer unique protein structures, higher mineral content, and a nutty depth.

    Spelt: the baker's friend

    Spelt has a delicate gluten that breaks down faster — handle gently. It adds a sweet, nutty flavour.

    Hydration adjustments

    Ancient grains absorb less water. Start with 5-10% less hydration.

    GrainProtein %water absorption
    Einkorn11-12%low
    Spelt13-15%medium
    Emmer12-14%medium-high
  • soak grains overnight
  • reduce mixing time
  • score deeper

    • FAQ

    Can I substitute 100% ancient grain?

    Yes, but expect denser crumb. Blend with bread flour for open structure.

    Baking with steam at home

    by M. Croft · 7 min read

    Steam is the secret to a glossy, crisp crust. In a home oven, use a cast iron pan with boiling water or a spray bottle.

    Methods

    • Dutch oven: traps steam naturally
    • Lava rocks + water
    • Ice cubes in a tray

    Add steam during the first 10 minutes, then vent for browning.
